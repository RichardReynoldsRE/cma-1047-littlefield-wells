Marketability Note
Alternative Use — Short-Term Lodging

The property has been operated as a short-term hospitality accommodation under the name "Maple Shadow Farm," available through major lodging marketplaces. This use does not change the property's residential fair-market value but is documented here for completeness regarding marketability.

What This Means for Value

Single-family residences with a documented short-term lodging history typically transact at residential market value — not at an income-capitalized hospitality value — because the buyer pool is dominated by primary-residence and second-home buyers, not commercial operators.

The lodging history does, however, expand the buyer pool to include second-home and snowbird buyers who value the option to defray ownership costs with occasional short-term rentals when the property is not in personal use. The use pattern is intermittent, not full-time rental.

No income-approach valuation has been applied. Wells municipal short-term rental rules, applicable lodging-tax treatment, and the absence of a multi-year stabilized P&L make capitalization unreliable for a property of this profile.
